# FODMAP Quick Reference Guide

Based on Monash University Research

## The Four FODMAP Categories

### 🧅 Oligosaccharides (Fructans & GOS)
**What they are**: Chains of fructose or galactose molecules

#### High FODMAP ❌
- **Grains**: Wheat, rye, barley
- **Vegetables**: Onions (all types), garlic, shallots, leeks (white part)
- **Legumes**: Chickpeas, beans, lentils, soybeans
- **Nuts**: Cashews, pistachios
- **Fruits**: Watermelon, white peaches, persimmons

#### Low FODMAP ✅
- **Grains**: Rice, quinoa, oats, corn, gluten-free products
- **Vegetables**: Green onion tops, garlic-infused oil (strained), bok choy, carrots, cucumber
- **Legumes**: Canned lentils (1/2 cup, rinsed), firm tofu
- **Nuts**: Macadamias, peanuts, pecans, walnuts
- **Tip**: Fructans are water-soluble, not fat-soluble (infused oils are safe!)

---

### 🥛 Disaccharides (Lactose)
**What they are**: Milk sugar (glucose + galactose)

#### High FODMAP ❌
- Fresh milk (cow, goat, sheep)
- Ice cream
- Yogurt (regular)
- Soft cheeses: Cottage cheese, ricotta, cream cheese, mascarpone
- Custard, pudding

#### Low FODMAP ✅
- **Lactose-free** milk, cream, yogurt
- **Hard cheeses**: Cheddar, Swiss, parmesan, brie, camembert
- **Butter** (very low lactose)
- **Greek yogurt** (lower lactose)
- **Portion tip**: Hard cheeses naturally low in lactose due to aging process

---

### 🍯 Monosaccharides (Excess Fructose)
**What they are**: Fruits with more fructose than glucose

#### High FODMAP ❌
- **Sweeteners**: Honey, agave nectar, high-fructose corn syrup
- **Fruits**: Apples, pears, mangoes, watermelon, cherries, dried fruit
- **Vegetables**: Asparagus, sugar snap peas
- **Juices**: Apple juice, pear juice

#### Low FODMAP ✅
- **Sweeteners**: Maple syrup, table sugar (sucrose), glucose, dextrose
- **Fruits**: Bananas, blueberries, strawberries, oranges, grapes, kiwi, pineapple
- **Tip**: Fructose is problematic when in excess of glucose; balance matters

---

### 🍎 Polyols (Sugar Alcohols)
**What they are**: Sugar molecules with hydroxyl groups (sorbitol, mannitol, etc.)

#### High FODMAP ❌
- **Artificial Sweeteners**: Sorbitol, mannitol, xylitol, maltitol, isomalt
- **Fruits**: Apples, apricots, avocados, blackberries, cherries, lychee, nectarines, peaches, pears, plums, prunes
- **Vegetables**: Mushrooms, cauliflower, snow peas, sweet corn
- **Products**: Sugar-free gum, diet products, "diabetic" foods

#### Low FODMAP ✅
- **Fruits**: Bananas, berries (most), citrus, grapes, kiwi, passion fruit
- **Vegetables**: Most vegetables (except those listed as high)
- **Sweeteners**: Regular sugar, glucose, maple syrup
- **Tip**: Often found in "sugar-free" products - always check labels!

## Portion Size Matters! 📏

Many foods are low FODMAP in **small amounts** but high in **large amounts**:

| **Food** | **Low FODMAP Serving** | **High FODMAP** |
|----------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Avocado | 1/8 fruit (20g) | > 1/4 fruit |
| Sweet corn | 1/2 cob | > 1 cob |
| Beetroot | 2 slices (20g) | > 2 slices |
| Brussels sprouts | 2 sprouts | > 2 sprouts |
| Butternut squash | 1/4 cup (30g) | > 1/2 cup |
| Broccoli | 3/4 cup (75g) | > 1 cup |
| Green beans | 15 beans | > 25 beans |
| Dried cranberries | 1 tbsp | > 1 tbsp |

**Key takeaway**: Respect serving sizes during elimination phase!

## Special Cases & Tips

### Green vs. White Parts
- **Spring onions/scallions**: Green tops ✅ LOW | White bulb ❌ HIGH
- **Leeks**: Green leaves ✅ LOW | White bulb ❌ HIGH

### Infused Oils
- **Garlic-infused oil** ✅ LOW FODMAP (if solids removed)
- **Onion-infused oil** ✅ LOW FODMAP (if solids removed)
- **Why?** FODMAPs are water-soluble, not fat-soluble

### Fermented Foods
- **Sourdough spelt bread** ✅ LOW FODMAP (properly fermented >4 hours)
- **Tempeh** ✅ LOW FODMAP (in moderation, 75g)
- **Kimchi** ⚠️ Varies (check ingredients - avoid garlic/onion)

### Cheese Aging
- **Fresh/soft cheese** ❌ HIGH (cottage, ricotta, cream cheese)
- **Aged/hard cheese** ✅ LOW (cheddar, parmesan, Swiss)
- **Why?** Lactose breaks down during aging

### Hidden FODMAPs
Always check labels for:
- Inulin / FOS
- High-fructose corn syrup
- Honey
- Agave
- Sorbitol, mannitol, xylitol
- Onion/garlic powder
- Wheat-based thickeners

## The Low FODMAP Diet Process

### Phase 1: Elimination (2-6 weeks)
- Eliminate all high FODMAP foods
- Follow LOW FODMAP diet strictly
- Allow gut to calm down
- Keep food diary

### Phase 2: Reintroduction (8-12 weeks)
- Test ONE FODMAP group at a time
- Start with small amount, increase over 3 days
- Rest 3 days between tests
- Identify personal triggers

### Phase 3: Personalization (Ongoing)
- Reintroduce all tolerated foods
- Avoid only proven triggers
- Maintain balanced diet
- Re-test periodically

## Important Reminders

### ⚠️ This is NOT:
- ❌ A weight loss diet
- ❌ A gluten-free diet (though they overlap)
- ❌ A lifetime restriction
- ❌ One-size-fits-all

### ✅ This IS:
- ✅ A diagnostic tool
- ✅ Temporary elimination followed by reintroduction
- ✅ Personalized to YOUR triggers
- ✅ Scientifically validated for IBS

### 👨‍⚕️ Professional Guidance
- **Always** work with a registered dietitian
- Get proper IBS diagnosis first
- Ensure nutritional adequacy
- Monitor for disordered eating patterns
- Don't self-diagnose

## Quick Troubleshooting

### "I'm still having symptoms"
- Check serving sizes (eating too much?)
- Look for hidden FODMAPs in processed foods
- Consider non-FODMAP triggers (caffeine, fat, stress)
- Consult your dietitian

### "Everything seems to trigger me"
- May not be FODMAPs alone
- Consider other conditions (SIBO, celiac, IBD)
- Check for non-dietary triggers
- Work with healthcare team

### "Can I eat out?"
- Yes! With planning
- Choose simple dishes
- Ask about ingredients
- Bring FODMAP app for reference
- Many restaurants now FODMAP-aware

## Emergency Substitution Guide

### Baking
- Wheat flour → Rice flour blend, gluten-free 1:1 flour
- Milk → Lactose-free milk, almond milk (small amounts)
- Honey → Maple syrup, golden syrup

### Cooking
- Onions → Green onion tops, asafoetida powder
- Garlic → Garlic-infused oil, ginger, chives
- Beans → Firm tofu, tempeh (75g), canned lentils (1/2 cup)

### Flavor Building
- Onion/garlic → Infused oils, herbs (basil, oregano, thyme)
- Stock with onion → Homemade stock with green tops
- Garlic powder → Asafoetida (hing) - use very sparingly!
